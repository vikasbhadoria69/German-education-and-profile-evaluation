Education in Germany



Germany is a diverse, exciting, constantly surprising country, and there's always something new to discover, even for people who have lived there for decades. From sports and history to language, music, food, culture and technology, there's so much to find out. There are some fun facts about this country that a person should know before visiting Germany, these include their obsession for reading, beer plays a big role in German life, bread is also a key symbol of German identity, Germans love Football, travel but the most important is their love and respect for thier language, history and culture. Germany is a small country with 16 federal states but the size dosen't matter when it comes to economy. German economy is really strong and their GDP is 4th largest in the world making it a financially strong nation. This is one of the reason why Germany has one of the best education system in the world. The government is capable to invest heavily in the education system as it considers education a the base of all developments in the nation. This makes Germany an ideal destination to pursue higher education as anyone would like to study in a well organized heavily funded education system. There are some key aspects of German education system which should be known before preparing for higher education. This artical will briefly cover some key information about higher studies in Germany and how can we help you to make your German dreams come real.


German Universities


The best part about German education is that it's free. Yes!!! the students do not have to pay anything and this is not just limited for Germans but also for the international students. Germany has public universities which are fully funded. They are further divided into :-

  • Technical Universities (Research-based study at universities)
  • Fach Hochschules( Universities of applied sciences)
    • While 90% of the universities are state funded some are also some private and it totaly depends on the student's profile which universities should be choosen. The thing to note is that private universities are at par with the government.

    Finding a study programme

    Bachelors Program: Bachelor's degree is the first university degree. The most common Bachelor's degrees are a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) and Bachelor of Engineering (B.Eng.). A Bachelor's degree is a fully-fledged university degree. The important thing to note here is that 95% of the time the Bachelors programms are in German making it very difficult for the international students to pursue them if they do not have prior knowledge of German. Students have to pass a studentkollege exam in Germany in addition to atleast C1 knowledge of German before starting their Bachelors.

    Masters Program: A Masters degree is the second university degree and is completed after a Bachelors degree. There are more then 105 universities in Germany providing Master's programm in English. These courses are free in public universities and the final degree is recognized all over the world be it for job search or PhD. The duration may vary depending upon the university. Mostly the duration is 2 years in Technical Universities and 1.5 years in Hochschules. ·There are number of professions that can be studied when it comes to masters. Management, Engineering, Pharmacy, Biotechnology, Economics, Finance and many more. The interesting part is the variety of courses that can be pursued under any of these professions. The "No Tution Fees" factor and the quality of education that too taught in English makes Germany one of the best destinations to pursue Masters.

    Doctorate(PhD): The doctoral degree ends with the awarding of the academic title of a doctorate. This involves the completion of a complex scientific research paper (dissertation). There are two paths that you can take: the traditional PhD model or the structured PhD programme. The traditional model involves graduating at a single faculty, supervised by a single professor. Many students prefer this path. By contrast, structured doctoral programmes are generally limited to three years and they include curricular specification and supervision by several scientists.

    General exams


    IELTS or TOEFL: Student can choose to give any of these two exams. It is an English test including four modules reading, listening, writing and speaking. A student should practice well for this exam. The validity of your IELTS, TOEFL score is for a period of 2 years.

    German Language Test.: Some Universities in Germany do ask for German A1 level even if the entire course is in English. Hence it is necessary to start learning German if the goal to pursue education in Germany is fixed. What level needed? Well atleast A1 is recommended but if there is enough time students should go ahead with A2. German certificates from Goethe institute are only recognized. Hence student can study anywhere but give exam only in Geothe. C1 is the level needed if student wants to apply for German taught course.

    GRE Test: This may be required by 2 or 3 universities for some particular courses. It may help student to build his overall profile. Students applying for engineering fields if gives GRE then they should focus more on Quants.

    Application Process

    Applications are either via UniAssist or directly to the university


    Application ways

  • UniAssist: An institute in Berlin which verify all the documents and then forward the application to the university. The fees to be paid is 75 euros for first application via UniAssit and 30 euros for the following applications.
  • Direct Applications: Are mostly online or via air mail. Free applications in 95% o the cases.

    • Application Documents

    10th and 12th certificates

    IELTS/TOEFL

    Transcripts along with grading system

    CV in tabular format

    2 academic LORs and one in case of work Ex

    SOP

    Degree certificate(bonafide certificate in case of degree not completed)

    After Applications

    Once the applications are done we then have to wait for the admits it will take 2 to 3 months depending on the university. Once the admits comes we can start the visa procress.

    Visa procress


    Important note

    The student to book a visa date at VFS Germany. The most important document is the Blocked Account Statement.

  • Bloacked account: The student has to block 10,236 euros in Kotak Mahindra Bank which has a tie up with German consulate. This can be done by going to any branch of Kotek Mahindra or can be done online at Fintiba

    • Documents required for Visa

    Passport

    Three biometric passport size photos

    Cover Letter

    Letter of admission from University

    IELTS/TOEFL

    Blocked account statement

    Academic qualifications

    Health and Travel insurance

    Proof of fee payment(in case of private university)

    After Applications

    Students get a temporary visa from India which will be extended in Germany by the International office of the respective university. Documents to be shown there are Blocked account statement, University enrollment and health insurance.

    Monthly expenses

  • 200-300 euros in rent, 100 euros for health insurance, 150-200 euros for daily necessities.
  • The expenses can vary from 450 to 650 euros depending upon the city.

    • Work Permit

  • Students in Germany are allowed to work 20 hours per week as a part time worker. Jobs outside the campus are also allowed. 10 euros/hour is a basic salary for everyone as a part time worker.
  • After Masters/Phd students are given an 18 months job search permit. The job applications are moslty online. There are no campus placements.
